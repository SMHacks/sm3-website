About

SM Hacks III is a high school hackathon that is aimed at high school students in the San Francisco Bay Area of all coding experience levels and backgrounds.

Our mission is to provide a safe, welcoming, diverse, and supportive space for students to explore the field of computer science.

FAQ

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is a 24-hour programming marathon where you bring your ideas to life! Over the course of the weekend, students from various programming backgrounds collaborate in teams of up to four to build something awesome. All participants must abide by the MLH Code of Conduct.

Do I have to be in high school?

Yes, only current high school students will be eligible to attend. Unfortunately, we are not accepting middle school students. College students are welcome to help out as mentors!

What if I don't have a team or an idea?

No worries! It is not uncommon for people to enter the hackathon without a prior group, and we'll make sure you're matched with the right people! There will be a team formation and brainstorm session the first night, so don't you worry first-time hackers!

How big can teams be?

Teams may consist of up to four people. While you may work individually, we highly encourage collaborating with others. If you don't have a team and need help finding one, don't miss our team formation and brainstorm session on Saturday.

What if I have another question?

Email us at team@smhacks.com if you have any additional questions. We are always happy to help!

What if I don't know how to code?

Not to worry! SM Hacks is the perfect time to learn how to code, and all students, regardless of skill level and experience, are welcome. There will be plenty of mentors glad to provide assistance, and we have workshops specifically designed for those without prior experience. Stay tuned to the schedule for more information regarding workshops!

How much does it cost to attend?

Nothing! Just bring your beautiful self and ideas, and we will provide you with the food, WiFi, workspace, and swag!

What should I bring?

Attendees will need a valid student ID or some other form of identification, as well as a completed and signed waiver. Bring laptops and chargers, along with any other technology that you may want to use! There will be designated sleeping spots so feel free to bring a sleeping bag and pillow. Also, it's highly recommended that you bring toiletries so you can feel refreshed and beautiful!
